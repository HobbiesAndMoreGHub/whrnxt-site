Half of a good trip happens before you leave. But most travel apps assume you're already *there* — they want your location, a signal, and a reason to nag you for permissions. WHRNXT doesn't.

## Pick a city, skip the GPS

Tap **Browse without location** and choose any city. No permission prompt, no "enable location to continue." You get the same hand-picked sights, food, and transit you'd see on the ground — measured from the city center instead of from you.

It's the fastest way to answer the questions you actually have weeks out:

- Is this neighborhood worth a full day, or an afternoon?
- What's near the place I'm staying?
- Which airport or station will I actually use?

## Turn scouting into a plan

Once a city looks promising, start dropping stops straight into a trip. Because everything is already measured and mappable, the planner can chain your picks into a sensible day the moment you're done browsing — no re-entering addresses later.

> Browsing is also just a nice way to daydream. Open a place you're curious about and see what's there. Telluride, Nantucket, Kyoto — they're all one tap away.

## It keeps working when the signal doesn't

Everything you browse is available offline, so the plan you built on your couch is still there on the plane, underground, or in a country where your data plan gave up. Nothing to sync, nothing to lose.
